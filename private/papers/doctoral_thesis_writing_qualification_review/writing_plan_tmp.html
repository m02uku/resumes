Writing Schedule Overview

The doctoral thesis will be completed over a 36-month period following a structured three-phase approach: Foundation (Year 1), Development (Year 2), and Completion (Year 3).

Year 1: Foundation Phase (Months 1-12)

Q1 (Months 1-3): Complete comprehensive literature review covering computational phonology, self-supervised learning, and neuro-symbolic integration. Draft theoretical framework chapter (40 pages).

Q2 (Months 4-6): Implement baseline models and evaluation framework. Complete methodology chapter (30 pages). Submit initial findings to computational linguistics workshop.

Q3 (Months 7-9): Conduct Study 1 on representational landscape analysis. Draft first empirical chapter (35 pages). Present preliminary results at departmental seminar.

Q4 (Months 10-12): Begin hybrid architecture development for Study 2. Write technical implementation documentation. Prepare conference submission for ACL or INTERSPEECH.

Year 2: Core Development Phase (Months 13-24)

Q1 (Months 13-15): Complete hybrid architecture implementation and evaluation. Draft second empirical chapter (40 pages).

Q2 (Months 16-18): Conduct comprehensive cross-linguistic evaluation. Refine theoretical contributions based on empirical findings.

Q3 (Months 19-21): Execute Study 3 on cognitive plausibility using CHILDES corpus. Draft third empirical chapter (35 pages). Submit journal article to Computational Linguistics.

Q4 (Months 22-24): Integrate all components and optimize performance. Complete preliminary draft of all empirical chapters. Present at international conference.

Year 3: Consolidation Phase (Months 25-36)

Q1 (Months 25-27): Conduct additional experiments to address gaps. Refine all empirical chapters based on feedback.

Q2 (Months 28-30): Write introduction (25 pages) and general discussion (30 pages) chapters. Complete first full dissertation draft.

Q3 (Months 31-33): Incorporate supervisor and committee feedback. Write conclusion chapter (20 pages). Prepare defense presentation.

Q4 (Months 34-36): Final revisions, formatting, and submission. Dissertation defense and required revisions.

Target Length and Structure

  • Total Length: 250-300 pages
  • Chapters: 8 (Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology, 3 Empirical Studies, Discussion, Conclusion)
  • Publications: Aim for 2-3 conference papers and 1 journal article during thesis period

Quality Assurance

  • Monthly progress meetings with supervisor
  • Quarterly committee reviews
  • Peer feedback through lab presentations
  • External collaboration for cross-validation

This structured approach ensures systematic progress while maintaining flexibility for unexpected findings and opportunities.
